Risk Disclosure

Important information about investment risks and potential losses

IMPORTANT RISK DISCLOSURE

All investments involve risk, including the possible loss of principal. Past performance is not indicative of future results.

General Investment Risks

Market Risk

  • Stock market volatility and price fluctuations
  • Economic downturns and recessions
  • Interest rate changes affecting valuations
  • Inflation reducing real returns
  • Geopolitical events and market disruptions

Liquidity Risk

  • Inability to sell investments quickly
  • Limited market for certain securities
  • Price discounts for rapid sales
  • Restricted or illiquid investments
  • Lock-up periods for alternative investments

Credit Risk

  • Bond issuer defaults
  • Credit rating downgrades
  • Counterparty failures
  • Corporate bankruptcies
  • Sovereign debt crises

Specific Investment Risks

Equity Investments

  • Company-specific business risks
  • Industry sector volatility
  • Management changes and corporate governance
  • Competitive pressures and market share loss
  • Regulatory changes affecting business models

Fixed Income Securities

  • Interest rate risk (inverse relationship)
  • Reinvestment risk for callable bonds
  • Default risk and credit deterioration
  • Inflation risk reducing real returns
  • Prepayment risk for mortgage-backed securities

Alternative Investments

  • Higher volatility and correlation risk
  • Limited transparency and reporting
  • Complex structures and strategies
  • Higher fees and expenses
  • Regulatory and legal uncertainties

Private Equity Risks

Private Equity Specific Risks

  • Illiquidity: Long lock-up periods (7-10 years)
  • Valuation Risk: Subjective and infrequent valuations
  • Concentration Risk: Limited number of investments
  • Leverage Risk: Use of debt amplifying losses
  • Exit Risk: Difficulty selling portfolio companies
  • Management Risk: Dependence on portfolio company management

Hedge Fund Risks

  • Strategy Risk: Complex investment strategies may fail
  • Leverage Risk: Use of borrowed magnifying gains and losses
  • Counterparty Risk: Exposure to broker and clearinghouse failures
  • Short Selling Risk: Unlimited loss potential
  • Derivatives Risk: Complex instruments with hidden risks
  • Transparency Risk: Limited disclosure of positions and strategies

Operational and Systemic Risks

Operational Risks

  • Technology failures and system outages
  • Cybersecurity breaches and data theft
  • Human error and internal fraud
  • Compliance failures and regulatory penalties
  • Business continuity disruptions

Systemic Risks

  • Financial system crises and contagion
  • Bank failures and credit freezes
  • Market circuit breakers and trading halts
  • Sovereign debt crises and defaults
  • Pandemic and natural disaster impacts

Risk Mitigation Strategies

Diversification

  • Asset class diversification
  • Geographic diversification
  • Sector diversification
  • Style diversification (growth vs. value)
  • Time diversification (dollar-cost averaging)

Risk Management

  • Regular portfolio rebalancing
  • Stop-loss orders and position limits
  • Stress testing and scenario analysis
  • Risk monitoring and early warning systems
  • Hedging strategies when appropriate

Investor Suitability

Accredited Investor Requirements

Many of our investment opportunities are available only to accredited investors who meet one of the following criteria:

  • Annual income exceeding $200,000 ($300,000 joint) for the last two years
  • Net worth exceeding $1,000,000 (excluding primary residence)
  • Professional knowledge and experience in financial matters
  • Hold certain professional certifications or designations

Risk Tolerance Assessment

Before investing, consider your:

  • Investment time horizon and liquidity needs
  • Financial situation and income stability
  • Risk tolerance and emotional response to losses
  • Investment knowledge and experience
  • Investment goals and objectives

Performance Expectations

Historical Performance

  • Past performance does not guarantee future results
  • Historical returns may not be achievable in the future
  • Market conditions change over time
  • Investment strategies may become less effective
  • Fees and expenses reduce actual returns

Return Expectations

  • Higher returns generally involve higher risk
  • No guarantee of positive returns
  • Potential for complete loss of principal
  • Tax implications affect after-tax returns
  • Inflation reduces purchasing power of returns

Regulatory Protections

SIPC Protection

  • Protects securities up to $500,000 per customer
  • Limited to $250,000 cash protection
  • Does not protect against market losses
  • Does not cover certain investment types
  • Available through SEC-registered broker-dealers

SEC Oversight

  • Regular examinations and inspections
  • Compliance with investment advisor regulations
  • Disclosure requirements and transparency
  • Client complaint procedures and resolution
  • Access to Form ADV and other disclosures

Questions to Ask

Before Investing

  • What are the specific risks of this investment?
  • How liquid is this investment?
  • What fees will I pay?
  • How has this investment performed historically?
  • Who is managing this investment?
  • What is the minimum investment amount?
  • How can I monitor my investment?
  • What is the exit strategy?
